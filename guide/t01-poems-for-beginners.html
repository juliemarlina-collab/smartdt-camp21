POEMS Observation
for Beginners

Empathise | Day 1 | CAMP21 Smart DT Project
1What is POEMS?

POEMS is a structured observation checklist. It helps beginners record what is really happening instead of guessing. You observe the situation through five lenses and turn what you see into evidence.

P · O · E · M · S = People · Objects · Environment · Messages · Services
2Observe vs Guess

✅ Observation — write this

“Visitor reads the sign twice.”
“Three people wait near the table.”
“She watches before touching the cloth.”

❌ Opinion — avoid this

“The sign is badly designed.”
“It looks chaotic.”
“She is nervous.”

Rule: record what your eyes can prove. Save judgement for later.

3The 5 Lenses

👥 People

Who is there? Roles, actions, emotions and interactions.

🧰 Objects

Tools, materials, products and items used.

🏠 Environment

Space, layout, movement and atmosphere.

💬 Messages

Signs, words, symbols, labels or stories seen/heard.

🤝 Services

Systems, counters, help, flow and support around the experience.

4How to Use It
1

Choose

Pick one real place or activity to observe.

2

Watch

Observe quietly for a set time. Do not interrupt.

3

Record

Write facts in every POEMS box.

4

Spot Patterns

Find what repeats and what surprises you.

5Beginner Tips
1

Write facts, not feelings.

2

Use your eyes first before asking questions.

3

Sketch or photograph only if permission is given.

4

One detail can fit more than one lens. That is fine.

6Case Study — Makcik, the Hall Cleaner
🧕🏽

People

Attendees leaving the hall; Makcik mopping alone; a colleague passing by.

Objects

Mop, bucket, floor cleaner, wet floor sign, chairs and tables.

Environment

Large, open hall; water spills and shoe marks after the programme.

Messages

Wet floor caution sign; schedule board showing the next event time.

💡 Insight: Makcik must balance speed and safety within a tight turnaround before the next group arrives.

“Observe first. Judge later.”
