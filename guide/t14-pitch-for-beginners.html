8-Slide Pitch Framework

Test  |  Phase 05  |  CAMP21 Smart DT Project
1 What is this template for?

Your pitch tells the story of your whole journey — problem, solution, testing and impact — in 8 short slides an audience can follow in a few minutes.

2 Clear vs Crowded
✅ Clear slide
"Problem: cleaners rush between programmes, risking slips."
One idea, one line, big enough to read.
❌ Crowded slide
Five paragraphs explaining every detail of the research.
Small text, multiple ideas fighting for attention.

Rule: if you have to shrink the font to fit it, you have too much text.

3 The 8 slides
1. Title & Teamproject name and who's presenting
2. Problemone line, from real evidence
3. Our Solutionone line describing the idea
4. How It Worksthe simplest explanation possible
5. Prototype & Testingwhat you built and who tested it
6. Impactwho benefits and how
7. Next Stepswhat you would do with more time
8. Thank Youclosing line or call to action
4 Four things every good pitch has
🎯

One Idea

Each slide makes exactly one point.

📖

A Story

Problem → solution → proof, in order.

🗣️

Real Evidence

Real quotes and test results, not guesses.

⏱️

Short & Timed

Practised to fit your time limit.

6 Beginner tips
1 Use your real T12 tester quote — it's more convincing than your own words.
2 Practice the transition between slides, not just the words on them.
3 End with a clear call to action, not just "thank you."
4 If a slide needs explaining out loud to make sense, simplify it.
7 Case study — Makcik, Hall Cleaner
🎤
Slide 2 — Problem"Hall cleaners rush between programmes, risking slips on wet floors."
Slide 3 — Solution"Colour-coded wet-zone markers that show estimated dry time."
Slide 5 — Testing"Tested with Makcik and a colleague — found the icon needed to be clearer."
Slide 8 — Closing"A small marker, a safer hall, for the person who cleans up after all of us."
💡 Why this works: every slide traces back to real evidence from earlier templates — nothing is made up for the pitch.
