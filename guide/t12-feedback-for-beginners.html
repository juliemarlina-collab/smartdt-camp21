1 What is this template for?

User testing puts your T11 prototype in front of a real person and records what actually happens — not what you hope will happen. One tester is enough to complete this template; test with more people if you have time.

2 Observe vs Assume
✅ Observed
"Tester paused for 3 seconds before touching the marker."
"Tester asked what the colour meant."
❌ Assumed
"Tester probably understood it fine."
"It looked easy to use."

Rule: write what you saw and heard, not what you think it means yet.

3 The form snapshot
Tester Codee.g. U01, not the tester's real name
Task Giventhe exact instruction given to the tester
What did you notice?what they did, said or got stuck on
Overall Ratingone score from 1–5 for how well it worked
Quotea direct line from the tester
Suggestionswhat the tester thinks could improve
4 Four things to watch for
👁️

Behaviour

What do they do, not just say?

😕

Confusion

Where do they pause or hesitate?

💬

Words

What exact words do they use?

⭐

Reaction

Are they relieved, frustrated, unsure?

5 Test it in 4 steps
1
Set the task

Give one clear task, then stay quiet.

›
2
Observe

Watch and write, don't explain or help.

›
3
Ask after

Only ask questions once they're done.

›
4
Record

Fill the form immediately, while it's fresh.

6 Beginner tips
1 Don't explain the prototype before they try it.
2 Silence is fine — let them think out loud.
3 Use tester codes (U01) instead of real names.
4 Test with at least 1 real person — more is great, but not required.
7 Case study — Makcik, Hall Cleaner
🧪
Task"Show me how you would use this marker after mopping."
ObservationMakcik picked up the marker but paused, unsure which colour meant "wet."
Overall Rating3/5 – Okay, some issues.
Quote"I like this, but I don't know which colour means wait and which means safe."
💡 Why this matters: a good idea can still fail if the colours aren't self-explanatory — this is real, useful evidence.
