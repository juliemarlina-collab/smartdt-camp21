User Insight & Needs

Define  |  Phase 02  |  CAMP21 Smart DT Project
1 What is an insight & needs statement?

It turns many interview findings into a few clear insights (what you learned) and the user's real needs (what would help them). It's how raw quotes become something your team can act on — without jumping to a solution.

2 The method
1
Gather

Write key quotes & observations.

›
2
Group

Cluster similar notes together.

›
3
Label

Name each cluster as a theme.

›
4
Formulate

Turn themes into insights & needs.

3 Clustering: from notes to themes
Low Awareness
"Young people don't know our products."
"Few visitors on weekdays."
Marketing
"I find it hard to promote online."
Price & Value
"Prices are compared with mass-produced items."
Loyalty
"I want repeat customers, not one-time buyers."

Group by meaning — what the notes are about — not by who said them.

4 The formula
[ User ] needs a way to [ Need ] because [ Insight ].
5 Insight vs Need
💡 Insight — what you learned
The seller has low visibility and needs steady footfall.
Customers value authenticity but are price-sensitive.
❤️ Need — what would help
More ways to attract weekday visitors.
Help communicating craft value and uniqueness.

An insight is a realisation; a need is what helps the user — neither is a solution yet.

6 Beginner tips
1 Listen more than you speak.
2 Group by meaning, not by speaker.
3 Insights come from the user, not assumptions.
4 A need is not a solution.
7 Case study — Makcik, Hall Cleaner
🧕🏽
Heard"This hall is big, it takes time to clean." · "I must finish before the next group arrives." · "I need the right tools."
ThemesTime pressure · Safety · Tools · Tight turnaround.
InsightLimited time between programmes forces Makcik to rush, which increases the risk of slips.
NeedsThe right tools, a realistic time gap, clear wet-floor signage, help moving furniture.
💡 Statement: Makcik needs the right tools and a realistic time gap between programmes to clean the hall safely and confidently.
“ Group by meaning. An insight is what you learned; a need is what helps. ”
