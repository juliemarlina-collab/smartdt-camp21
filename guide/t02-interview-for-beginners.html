Interview Guide
for Beginners

Empathise | Day 1 | CAMP21 Smart DT Project
1What is an interview guide?

An interview guide helps your team ask the right questions, listen carefully, and collect useful stories, actions, thoughts and feelings from users. It keeps the conversation focused while still allowing natural sharing.

2What to Prepare

🎯 Goal

Know what your team wants to learn.

💬 Questions

Prepare open-ended questions, not leading questions.

👥 Roles

One person asks, one person records notes.

📓 Materials

Notebook, recorder, consent and evidence if allowed.

3Interview Flow
1

Introduce Yourself

Greet the user and share who you are.

2

Introduce Project

Explain the purpose and why their input matters.

3

Build Rapport

Show respect and help the user feel safe.

4

Ask for Stories

Invite experiences, examples and real moments.

5

Explore Emotions

Ask how they felt and what mattered.

6

Follow Up + Wrap

Clarify, thank them and close well.

4Question Starters

❝ Experience

Can you tell me about your experience when you tried this?

❝ Action

What usually happens when you join this activity?

❝ Feeling

How did you feel at that moment?

❝ Why

Why was that important or difficult for you?

5Beginner Tips
1

Ask open-ended questions.

2

Start simple to break the ice.

3

Let the user tell stories before you interrupt.

4

Record exact words when possible.

6Case Study — Makcik, the Hall Cleaner

🧕🏽 Situation

A student interviews Makcik while she cleans the hall after a programme.

💬 Good Questions

“What part of cleaning this hall takes the most time?”
“What worries you most while mopping?”

👁️ Observation

Makcik checks the wet floor sign often and keeps glancing at the schedule.

⭐ Key Insight

Makcik needs the right tools and enough time to finish safely before the next group arrives.

👂 Reminder: Listen more than you speak. The goal is to understand the user, not test your own idea.
